About me

I am a Geophysicist, holding a degree from UNLP, currently contributing to the Marine Geology and Geophysics division at SHN. My expertise extends beyond traditional geophysics, encompassing a diverse scope of applications within this field. Since 2021, I've held a significant role at SHN, simultaneously teaching Mathematics and Physics across multiple faculties at UNLP and the University of Almirante Brown. My approach to geophysics spans various facets, leveraging Python as a primary tool in conjunction with daily utilization of collaborative platforms like Trello, Github, and other open-source resources. At SHN, I helm the geophysics section and actively collaborate on research projects within the GEOMARGEN group at the Oceanography Department. Additionally, I've spearheaded and contributed to numerous extension university projects, overseeing team efforts, organizing educational events, such as the Earth Week at the Planetarium of La Plata, and adeptly managing project budgets. Beyond my professional pursuits, I find solace and passion in diverse interests. Formerly an avid chess player, I once engaged in competitive tournaments, although my current commitments have led me to scale back. I indulge in following tennis and diving into books, exploring the realms of finance through podcasts, news, and researching stocks, bonds, and cryptocurrency ventures.
